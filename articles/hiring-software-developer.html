How to Hire a Software Developer for Your Startup

By Kaan Guluzada • 10 Min Read

Finding the right tech partner is one of the most critical decisions for a founder. Whether you're looking to build an MVP for a startup or modernize a legacy web application, the developer you choose will directly impact your speed to market and product quality.

1. Freelance Software Developer vs. Agency

The first question is usually: should I hire a freelance developer or a large agency?

Agencies offer a full team but come with high overhead costs and slower communication loops. You're often paying for account managers and office space, not just code.

A dedicated freelance software developer (like myself) offers a direct line of communication. You work with the person writing the code. This is often the best choice for startups that need to move fast and iterate quickly.

2. Choosing the Right Tech Stack

Don't get lost in buzzwords. For most modern web applications, the "best" stack is one that is popular, well-supported, and scalable.

  • Frontend: React.js is the industry standard. It's fast, modular, and has a huge ecosystem.
  • Backend: Node.js allows for unified JavaScript development across the full stack.
  • Database: PostgreSQL or MongoDB offer flexibility and performance.

Hiring a Full Stack React Node.js developer ensures you have one expert who can handle the entire application, reducing coordination costs.

3. How Much Does it Cost to Build a Web Application?

Pricing varies wildy. A simple marketing site might cost $1k-$5k, while a custom SaaS application can range from $10k to $50k+.

To keep costs down, focus on the MVP (Minimum Viable Product). Identify the 2-3 core features that solve your user's primary problem. Don't build "nice-to-haves" until you have paying customers.

4. What to Look for in a Portfolio

When reviewing a software developer portfolio website, look for:

  • Case Studies: Do they explain the problem and the solution, not just show pretty pictures?
  • Performance: Does their own website load fast? (Check their PageSpeed).
  • Live Links: Can you test the actual applications they built?
